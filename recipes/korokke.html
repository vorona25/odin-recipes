Japanese Croquette (Korokke)

Description

Japanese Potato Croquettes, or “Korokke” as they are called in Japanese, is a delicious fried food made from panko-crumbed mashed potato with carrot, onion, and mince. In Japan, these are tasty street food but are also one of those Japanese home-style dishes that can easily be made from scratch.

Korokke is the Japanese name for potato croquettes. The outside of Korokke is perfect golden-brown that gives a wonderful “Crunch!” sound as you bite into it. And inside is the perfect “hoku hoku” mashed potato with the ultimate ratio of potato to beef mince.

Japanese Potato Croquettes are served either for lunch or dinner because this little croquette is like an all-in-one-meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb/500g Starchy Potatoes
  • 0.3 lb/120g Ground Beef
  • 0.15lb/70g Onion
  • 0.15/7-g Carrot
  • 1/2 tbs olive oil
  • Deep Frying Oil
  • 1 egg
  • 4 tbs plain flour
  • 3 tbs water
  • 1 tbs kewpie mayonnaise
  • 2 cups of Japanese panko bread crumb

Steps

  1. Peel and Dice the potatoes
  2. Cook potatoes in boiling water about 10 minutes
  3. While potato being cooked, finely chop onion and carrot and set aside
  4. Heat olive oil in frying pan over high heat and add mince to cook
  5. When most of ground beef changes color, Add carrot and onion to cook about 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper, then set aside
  6. Check if potatoes are cooked using skewer or fork. Potatoes cooked if it goes through cleanly
  7. Strain water from potatoes, Put back in pot and shake them to rough up outsides a little
  8. Mash potatoes with potato masher while potato is warm
  9. Add cooked ground beef, onion, and carrot to mashed potatoes, Combine well
  10. Divide the mix into 8 equal sized flat ovals
  11. Combine egg, water, mayonnaise, and flour in a small bowl to make batter
  12. Coat each of the ovals with batter, then place potato oval in pank cumbs
  13. Coat all potato ovals with pank crumbs
  14. Heat deep frying oil in a deep fan until it reaches around 350 F or 180 C
  15. Fry each of potato ovals until crispy and golden brown
  16. Serve croquettes with salad leaves or any side dish of choice
